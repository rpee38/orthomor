Orthographic, grapho-phonological, and morphological characteristics

Manulex_Infra (ver. 2) and Manulex_Morpho (ver. 2) are two databases derived from Manulex, a web-accessible database listing word-frequency values for about 49.000 lexical entries encountered in 54 French elementary-school readers. Their development was motivated by the observation that analyses of child-directed written material were limited to statistical estimates of word occurrence. In spite of the undeniable relevance of the word-frequency norms for studying literacy acquisition, word frequency is obviously not the sole factor influencing reading and writing performance, so more specialized databases are needed. In particular, it is well known that the statistical characteristics of a language, such as the consistency of the mappings between orthography and phonology, influence literacy acquisition.

The aim of Manulex_infra was thus to provide quantitative estimates for several infra-lexical variables (syllable, grapheme-to-phoneme mappings, bigrams), and lexical variables (lexical neighborhood, homophony and homography). Unlike Manulex_infra, the morphological structure of the written words is also considered in Manulex_morpho when computing the consistency of orthography-phonology mappings (inflectional morphology, silent word-ending pronounced in inflected/derived forms). These analyses should permit quantitative descriptions of the written language in beginning readers, the manipulation and control of variables based on objective data in empirical studies, and the development of instructional methods in keeping with the distributional characteristics of the orthography.

The current version of these two databases is v.2. Several corrections have been made to previous versions.
